# ScotiTech Public Website Copy Rules

Public copy must sound customer-facing, confident, and product-led. Do not use internal validation language such as "product proof", "mobile product proof", "positioning", "commercial story", "surface", "hurdle", or "defensive".

Preferred tone:

- Say what the visitor can use, evaluate, or trust.
- Use product outcomes, buyer context, and concrete capabilities.
- Keep headings polished and direct.
- Use "workspace", "experience", "preview", "release story", "launch path", or "portfolio snapshot" instead of internal proof language.
- Mention constraints or disclaimers only when they help users understand scope, safety, or access.
